//Summary                                                                                                                                                                 Summary                                Ritsuko is Senior Research Fellow in the Department of Innovation and Entrepreneurship, and is Visiting Research Fellow at the Institute of Industrial Science, the University of Tokyo, and the Policy Studies Institute, the University of Westminster. She holds a DPhil in Sociology from the University of Sussex. Prior to joining the Business School, she worked at SPRU (Science Policy Research Unit), the University of Sussex. During her time at SPRU, her research centred around innovative housebuilding in the UK. Her research interests are: innovations for sustainaiblity in the urban built environment context; adoption and diffusion of new energy technologies and infrastructures; and user-technology interactions, with a particular focus on social practices. She currently is involved in the following research projects:CIVIS - Cities as Drivers of Social Changes: This project is funded by the European Commission's 7th Framework Programme and is a collaboration of 12 European institutes. her work looks into how ICT-based community-led initiatives can promote energy efficient behaviours and low-carbon neighbourhoods (http://www.civisproject.eu/).Digital City Exchange: In this College-wide Smart City project her work package looks into user perceptions of and engagement with digital technologies that are designed to help improve users' lives, such as e-car sharing, and their diffusion (http://www3.imperial.ac.uk/digital-economy-lab/partnernetworks/dce).EPSRC Energy Networks Grand Challenge Top & Tail Transformation: This is a collaboration of eight UK universities, led by Prof Tim Green (Dept of Electrical and Electronic Engineering). Ritsuko's work package explores professional users' understanding and perception of new energy infrastructures (in particular, LV networks) (http://www.topandtail.org.uk/).Furthermore, she is part of the EPSRC Centre for Innovative Manufacturing in Industrial Sustainability, a collaboration of four UK universities and several industy partners, led by Cambridge University (http://www.industrialsustainability.org/).She will also be working with the National Institute for Envrionmental Studies in Japan (http://www.nies.go.jp/) to look into city-level infrastructures for sustainability and the frameworks to support such infrastructures and their diffusion from 2016.She is Co-ordinater of the Business School's Energy Business Research Laboratory: http://www3.imperial.ac.uk/energy-business-research.                                                                                     Publications
