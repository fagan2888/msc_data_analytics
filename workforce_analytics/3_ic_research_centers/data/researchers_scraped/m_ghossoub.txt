//Summary                                                                                                                                                                 Summary                                Dr. Mario Ghossoub is an an Associate of the Society of Actuaries (ASA), a Chartered Enterprise Risk Ananlyst (CERA), a Financial Risk Manager (FRM) - certified by the Global Association of Risk Professionals (GARP), and an Assistant Professor of Finance (tenure track) at Imperial College Business School. He teaches in the MSc Actuarial Finance and MSc Risk Management and Financial  Engineering programmes.His research is focused on the applications of the modern theory of choice under uncertainty and ambiguity to insurance, risk management, and quantitative finance, with emphasis on the effect of ambiguity and belief heterogeneity on optimal insurance design and portfolio choice. Mario's research has attracted funding from the Society of Actuaries, the Social Sciences and Humanities Research Council of Canada, the Natural Sciences and Engineering Research Council of Canada, the Waterloo Research Institute in Insurance, Securities and Quantitative Finance, and the Institute for Quantitative Finance and Insurance at the University of Waterloo.Prior to joining Imperial College London in August 2013, Mario was a SSHRC postdoctoral fellow in the Department of Economics at the University of Montreal, and a visiting postdoctoral fellow in the Center of Mathematical Studies in Economics and Management at Northwestern University. He is a former Fulbright Scholar and a former Hickman Scholar. He holds a PhD in Actuarial Science from the University of Waterloo and a MS in Mathematics from the University of Michigan.
